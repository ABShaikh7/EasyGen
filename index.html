    The Prompt: Give me an approximate idea of how networks run through wires to provide houses with internet

    How each AI answered:

      Gemini: Internet data travels as light through fiber-optic backbones and converts to electrical signals over local wires to reach your home modem.

      DeepSeek: Fiber is fastest, coaxial is common for cable, and copper phone lines (DSL) are slower but widely available.

      ChatGPT: The internet sends data through cables as electrical signals or light pulses from providers to your home router.

      Copilot: Your home internet is just a chain of cables and devices linking you to the wider network.
